Investment Thesis

Since 2008, global liquidity expansion — driven by central bank balance sheet growth and quantitative easing — has been one of the dominant forces behind risk asset appreciation. Within this environment, the technology sector has captured a disproportionate share of value creation, particularly through AI infrastructure, digital transformation, and productivity gains.

The Tech Megatrend Tactical ETI seeks to capture the upside of this structural liquidity environment while actively managing downside risk through a discretionary long-or-cash approach.

Strategy

The strategy uses a rules-based timing methodology centered on global liquidity and quantitative easing signals. The model identifies periods of expanding liquidity (constructive for risk assets) and periods of tightening or liquidity stress (higher risk regimes).

  • Long positions in highly liquid leveraged technology ETFs (TECL, TQQQ, QQQ) during favorable liquidity regimes
  • Shifts to cash during periods of liquidity contraction or elevated systemic risk
  • Low trading frequency (typically 0–6 major signals per year)
  • Focus on capturing the main thrust of QE-driven bull markets while avoiding major drawdowns

Risk Management

The primary risk management tool is the discretionary shift to cash during periods of elevated risk, as identified by the QE/global liquidity model. This approach has historically helped reduce exposure during major market dislocations compared to passive leveraged technology exposure.

Investors should note that leveraged ETFs carry substantial volatility. The fund’s value lies in actively managing this exposure rather than eliminating it.
